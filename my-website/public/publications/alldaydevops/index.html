Motifs Discovery in Time Series, Application

Link to full Talk

This presentation covers a range of methods for analyzing and discovering motifs in time series, from traditional approaches to modern, advanced techniques. It compares various similarity measurement formulas like Euclidean distance, highlighting their strengths and limitations. The presentation discusses how some methods may fail in certain cases and contrasts their effectiveness in generating high-quality results. It also addresses the inefficiency of traditional approaches in the era of big data and proposes alternative solutions for better analysis.

  • Traditional vs. modern methods for time series analysis
  • Comparison of similarity measurements (e.g., Euclidean distance)
  • Strengths and weaknesses of different formulas
  • Quantitative vs. qualitative results in time series discovery
  • Limitations of traditional methods in the age of big data
  • Alternative approaches to improve analysis performance
